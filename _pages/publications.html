My research examines how democratic representation is shaped by the interaction of public opinion, political institutions, and policymaking in American politics. Alongside my dissertation research, I maintain an active pipeline of collaborative and independent projects examining public opinion differences among social groups, primary and general election voter turnout, and state politics and policy diffusion. I have also contributed to multiple faculty-led research assistantships, employing skills like quantitative data collection, voter file analysis, survey research, and large-scale policy coding. Collectively, these experiences have strengthened both my substantive interests and methodological training while allowing me to develop a broad and evolving research portfolio.
